DPE Enrollment Process — 4 Steps
1
Select Program and Provider
Begin enrollment in the DPE app at dpe.autopartners.net. Select the KBB ICO program and provider.
2
Contact Provider for Product Selection
In the Product Selection step, contact information for your provider is displayed. They've already been notified of the dealer's decision to enroll. Contact the provider or wait for outreach — let them know which products were selected. They process it into the DPE and enrollment is automatic.
The DPE updates product information based on when it receives data from providers. Providers are encouraged to send updates at least once per day.
3
Review Product Selections in the DPE
After the DPE receives product info from the provider, it sends an enrollment email. The dealer reviews selections in the Product Selection step of their program in the DPE.
If there are pricing or product discrepancies, contact the provider. They send updates to the DPE where they're automatically applied.
✓
Finalize Enrollment
After product selection is confirmed, enrollment is automatically finalized. No further action needed from the dealer.
Compliance: Retail contracting outside GM programs is a contract breach for GM-eligible dealers. GM and KBB audit dealer websites and monitor SKU usage. Improper enrollment can result in appraisal tools being disabled and BTS remediation.

Seller Talking Points — GM Program
🏭
Four Brands, One Program
Eligibility
All GM-franchised dealers — Chevrolet, Buick, GMC, and Cadillac — are eligible under the same OEM program structure, SKUs, and enrollment path. Don't treat them as separate programs.
"If you're a Chevrolet, Buick, GMC, or Cadillac dealer, KBB appraisal tools must be enrolled through GM's OEM program to stay compliant."
⚙
Enrollment Is Through the DPE Portal
Enrollment Process
GM dealers enroll inside the GM Dealer Program Enrollment (DPE) app at dpe.autopartners.net. Once the dealer selects KBB, ops handles provisioning. Enrollment is 4 steps and automatically finalized — send the dealer the DPE Quick Start before your meeting.
"Enrollment happens inside the GM portal. Once you select KBB there, our ops team handles setup."
⬆
EA Is the Upgrade, Lead Driver Is the Add-On
Product Strategy
OEM Appraisal is the GM-approved baseline. Expert Appraisal ($849/mo) is the dealer-paid upgrade for stronger acquisition. Lead Driver ($149/mo) is a separate product for web lead capture — dealers frequently take both. Total commitment: $998/mo for the full suite.
📈
GM-Specific Performance Stats
Value Proposition
Use GM-specific stats in your pitch: 4% lower cost-to-market on ICO-acquired vehicles (vs. non-ICO), and 46%+ look-to-book when KBB ICO is used. These are stronger numbers than the general KBB stats and more credible with GM dealers.
⚠
Compliance Is Actively Enforced
Compliance
GM and KBB audit dealer websites and monitor SKU usage. Retail contracting outside GM programs is a contract breach. Improper enrollment can result in appraisal tools being disabled, contract rework, and BTS remediation. Always use GM-approved SKUs.
"If a GM dealer isn't enrolled correctly through GM, the appraisal experience can be turned off."
✔
What Proper Enrollment Gets Them
Value Add
When correctly enrolled through GM: GM-approved KBB trade-in experience, continued access to the KBB brand, optional upgrade path to Expert Appraisal, and alignment with GM digital retailing standards. Frame compliance as protection, not restriction.
